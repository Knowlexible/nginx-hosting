  • Quack Application - (Android Studio)

    • Purpose & Goals:
         Allow users to discover new websites based off their interests

    • Easy Use - Make the user feel familiar to the overall design
    • Personalized - Allows the user to explore different interests of theirs
    • Sharable - Users would be able to share unknown websites to one another


    About:

        The above photo displays the process of how my design was altered throughout our project.

    • Stage 1 there was focus on following the general layouts of the app overall.
    • Stage 2 was the design that occurred after implementing our code into Adobe XD and testing the application by turning the parts of the design into assets.
    • Stage 3 was the final design that came from our goal.



    The Outcome:

        In the final outcome of the application there were five activities that would occur when the user opened our application. First, they would be brought to a login page where they would enter their necessary credentials. Once entering their data they would then click the login button which would then bring them to the screen displayed next where they would choose a circle button of what exactly they were looking for. Once choosing their interest it would bring them to the main activity as seen above on the right where it would display a randomized url. If the user wanted to change their choice again then they would be brought to the two middle activities on the right.


  • Adobe Photoshop Designs


    • Photo Recreation: Cafe

    In this composition, the goal was to be able to recreate an image found online and turn it into a painting. First, I did all of the linework using Illustrator, afterwards, I then transferred the linework into Photoshop where I then shaded the piece to be as realistic as possible.

    Frankenstein Animal

    In this piece, the goal was to create a fantasy-like creature using images of other animals from Google; the animal was created from a lion, alaskan malamute, goat, goose, and a lemur. Due to the animal having a thick coat, I decided on placing it in a cold environment on the mountains. To help better the overall look of the creature, I decided to place a blue tint on top of the animal to help blend it in by messing with the transparency tool.

    Tree Loss Infographic

    The infographic focuses mainly on the Top 10 Countries facing tree loss. I formed a tree out of the top countries that led to the most tree loss destruction and made sure the color palette was dreary.


  • Adobe Illustrator


    • Blood Drive Flyer
    Bake Sale Flyer
    Thanks4Giving Blood Drive & Bake Sale

    I was the Marketing/Advertising Chair for project Thanks4Giving in which I was in charge of nine other members and had to work with four other chairs (e.g. financing, baking, blood drive, and project manager). I was also in charge of designing flyers and these were the two finalized designs that my team approved as well as the rest of the other teams.     Lost in Shapes

    I would create random shapes using the line tool and place them on top of each other seeing what design would work best. The most difficult part would have to be layering the shapes as many of the shapes are fluid. The design reminds me of camo print. As for future projects similar to this one, I would say that I would try doing the piece in a variety of colors to experiment which color combinations would work best with one another.

    Butterflies

    Began with a series of sketches and then I transferred my designs into Adobe. In order to help the piece be more unified, I decided on creating flowers that looked like daisies to help the design feel less empty. Afterwards, I placed the butterflies in various layouts and then copied and pasted until the designs formed a pattern resulting in the image above.

    LALA

    This typography piece was centered around the theme of playfulness; I thought it was rather fitting to use the two letters "L" and "a" to create lala. During the time that I made this composition I was still obsessed with the movie, "La La Land" so that influenced the composition overall. In order to make the letter appear shaded, I used a series of l's and vertically placed them on each of the big letters to make them pop.



    Illusions

    The challenge for this project was to create a composition with a given set of shapes; I decided to do a design that reminded me of the platforms from the old Mario games. My main focus in this piece was to make sure that there was a lot of movement as well as twists and turns.

    Red and Black

    This design required me to think outside of the box because you could only use the one shape that you created and had to make sure that the negative space was unified with your design. I do believe that I could have made sure the linework was much more smoother. Having to find a way to make sure that the red and black were not too overpowering also proved to be a challenge.

    3 Black Shapes

    I first folded a piece of paper in half and then only drew a random shape on one side. Afterwards, I cut out the shape so that both sides of the paper were symmetrical. I then took a picture of the resulting shape and then used the pen tool in Illustrator to create the resulting composition.
